# Office Hours Protocol

Reference for gstack-office-hours YC-style product diagnostic and brainstorming.

## Two Modes

| Mode | Trigger | Purpose |
|------|---------|---------|
| Startup | Product idea, new venture, market question | Six forcing questions exposing demand reality |
| Builder | Side project, hackathon, design exploration | Design thinking brainstorm for builders |

## Startup Mode: Six Forcing Questions

Ask sequentially. Each must be answered before proceeding.

1. **Demand reality** — Who is desperate for this? Name a specific person or company.
   Not "developers" but "a solo founder who just lost 3 days to X."
2. **Status quo** — What do they do today without your product? How painful is it?
   If the answer is "nothing" or "it's fine," demand is weak.
3. **Desperate specificity** — What exact moment makes them say "I need this NOW"?
   The trigger event, not the chronic condition.
4. **Narrowest wedge** — What is the smallest version that solves the desperate moment?
   Not the platform vision. The single thing that makes one person switch.
5. **Observation** — What have you seen (not assumed) that others have missed?
   First-hand evidence, not market reports. What did you notice that made you think
   "wait, that's wrong"?
6. **Future-fit** — Why does this get stronger over time, not weaker?
   Network effects, data moats, switching costs, or compounding value.

## Builder Mode: Design Thinking Brainstorm

1. Understand the builder's context (what they have, what they want)
2. Diverge: generate 5-10 directions without filtering
3. Converge: rank by feasibility x impact for a solo builder
4. Pick one direction and define the narrowest first version
5. Identify the single riskiest assumption and how to test it in < 1 week

## Validation Framework

After either mode, assess:

| Dimension | Strong Signal | Weak Signal |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| Demand | Named person desperate today | "People might want..." |
| Wedge | One feature, one persona, one trigger | Platform with 10 features |
| Observation | First-hand, surprising, specific | Market report, trend article |
| Timing | Structural shift enabling this now | "AI makes everything possible" |
| Moat | Gets harder to replicate over time | Feature that anyone can copy |

## Output

Produce a structured summary with:
- Problem statement (one sentence)
- Target persona (specific, named archetype)
- Narrowest wedge (the MVP that solves the desperate moment)
- Riskiest assumption + validation plan
- Go/no-go recommendation with reasoning
